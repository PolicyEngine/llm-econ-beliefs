For the requested direct adjudication: the permutation calculation is sound for finite, nondegenerate data—`|rho|` is a conventional two-sided statistic, the fixed seed is reproducible, the Monte Carlo `+1` correction is correct, and full enumeration correctly uses `hits/n!` without `+1`. Twenty thousand draws are adequate for exploratory work but do not justify four-decimal precision. `rank_map()` and `spearman()` use consistent exact-equality, tie-averaged ranks; the inconsistent pieces are presentation-rounding, dashboard ordinal ranks, and the missing Kruskal–Wallis tie correction.

**Verdict:** This is not safe to integrate in its current state. After locking and atomic recovery, a uniform fresh GLM run, balanced-panel validation, raw one-way statistical inputs, corrected/disclosed inference, audited joins/provider taxonomy, and complete paper/dashboard regeneration, it can support a peer-reviewed pipeline. The permutation core itself is broadly sound; the blocking risks are data integrity, protocol selection, stale build dependencies, and overstated inferential/publishing integration.
