# Referee Report — RSOS Referee 2 (Public-Finance Economist)

**Overall Recommendation**

`Major revision`

This is a careful, honest descriptive paper, and on the soundness criteria that govern RSOS the underlying data collection (17 models, 15 repeated runs, structured outputs, 100% parse rate) is solid and well documented. The public-finance content has also improved relative to the earlier rounds: the ETI-to-top-rate exercise now carries a non-zero welfare weight and propagates elicited uncertainty into the implied rate, and there is a new Pareto/CRRA robustness table and a genuine capital-gains convention audit. My recommendation for major revision rests on four correctable but non-cosmetic soundness problems: (1) the headline optimal-tax exhibit in the text is computed with a different Pareto parameter than the committed tables, so the reported top-rate numbers do not reproduce; (2) the welfare-weight normalization behind the "utilitarian" label is nonstandard and under-described, and the caveat is inadequate; (3) several benchmark ranges are attributed to sources that do not state them, and those sources are absent from the bibliography; and (4) several descriptive claims in the abstract and body are contradicted by the paper's own tables.

**Major Comments**

**1. The optimal-top-tax exhibit in the text is calculated with a different Pareto parameter than the committed Table 4 and Table A13, and the text's headline rates do not reproduce from the artifacts.** The text states the Pareto parameter is microdata-calibrated to `a = 1.470` (from a top-1% AGI threshold of `$659,618` and tail mean `$2,062,980`; I confirm `b = 2{,}062{,}980/659{,}618 = 3.128` and `a = b/(b-1) = 1.470`), giving `ḡ = a/(a+1) ≈ 0.595` and the mapping `τ* = 0.405/(0.405 + 1.470 e)` ([paper.qmd:231](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:231), [paper.qmd:234](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:234), [paper.qmd:240](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:240)). But the committed Table 4 was generated with a **fallback** `a = 1.500` and `ḡ = 0.600` — the note says so explicitly ([toy-top-rate-labor-tax.md:1](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/toy-top-rate-labor-tax.md:1)) — and Table A13's note states its baseline column is `a = 1.500` and "reproduces the median column in Table 4" ([top-rate-robustness.md:1](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/top-rate-robustness.md:1)). The consequences are concrete and verifiable:
   - The text reports the top-rate median ranging from `33.5%` (GPT-5.4 nano) to `44.3%` (Gemini 3.1 Pro), with GPT-5.4 at `39.6%`, a `10.8` pp spread ([paper.qmd:247](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:247)). Table 4 shows `32.8%`, `43.2%`, `38.8%`, a `10.4` pp spread ([toy-top-rate-labor-tax.md](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/toy-top-rate-labor-tax.md)). The text figures match `a = 1.470` (e.g., GPT-5.4 nano: `0.405/(0.405+1.470·0.546)=33.5%`); the table figures match `a = 1.500` (`0.400/(0.400+1.500·0.546)=32.8%`). None of the three headline percentages in the text appear in the table.
   - Table A13's shift magnitudes cannot be reconciled with the text. The text says moving `a` from "the baseline 1.470" to 1.3 shifts rates up by `4.5`–`4.8` pp and to 1.7 down by `5.0`–`5.7` pp, with the `γ=2` weight falling from `0.595` to `0.424` ([paper.qmd:249](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:249)). But recomputing from the published A13 baseline (`a=1.500`) gives up-shifts of `5.2`–`5.6` pp (e.g., nano `32.8→38.0`), down-shifts of `4.3`–`4.9` pp (e.g., Gemini 3.1 Pro `43.2→38.3`), and a `γ=2` weight of `0.429`, as A13's own note states ([top-rate-robustness.md:1](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/top-rate-robustness.md:1)). A reader who tries to verify the robustness narrative from the table will get different numbers.

   The word "fallback" in the Table 4 note strongly suggests the microdata Pareto calibration described in the text did not actually flow into the artifact build (it fell back to a hard-coded `1.500`). This is a complete-reporting / conclusions-supported-by-data failure on the paper's single most policy-relevant exhibit. It must be fixed by regenerating Table 4 and A13 with the calibrated `a = 1.470` (and `ḡ = 0.595`; `γ=2` weight `0.424`), or by correcting all text and the footnote to `a = 1.500` — and by making the footnote, the Table 4 note, and the A13 note state one consistent value.

**2. The welfare-weight normalization is nonstandard and under-described, and the "utilitarian" label produces optimal rates far below the textbook utilitarian optimum without an adequate caveat.** The paper sets `ḡ = a/(a+γ) ≈ 0.595` and calls the resulting rates "utilitarian" ([paper.qmd:231](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:231)–[paper.qmd:234](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:234)). Under a Pareto tail and CRRA utility, `a/(a+γ)` is `E[u'(c) | top] / u'(z*)` — the average marginal utility of top earners **normalized to the marginal utility of the household at the top-bracket threshold** (`z* = $660k`). That is a defensible "relative-to-threshold" object, but it is *not* the standard utilitarian benchmark, in which the marginal value of public funds equals the population-average marginal utility. Under the standard normalization the social marginal welfare weight on the top is `ḡ_top → 0`, and the optimal top rate is `τ* = 1/(1 + a e)` — roughly `1/(1+1.470·0.42) = 61.8%` for GPT-5.4, versus the paper's `~39%`. The choice of `ḡ ≈ 0.6` mechanically halves the implied optimal rate relative to the Diamond–Saez utilitarian optimum, yet the paper never states the normalization (relative to what?) and never flags that the level is an artifact of this choice rather than of the elicited ETI. The caveats that are present ([paper.qmd:243](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:243)) address the ETI-only nature and the truncation but not the welfare-weight normalization. For a public-finance reader, the "utilitarian" label as used is misleading. Please (a) state the normalization explicitly, (b) add the standard `ḡ_top → 0` (revenue-maximizing / Diamond–Saez) rate as a reference column so the level sensitivity is visible, and (c) either qualify or relabel "utilitarian."

**3. Several benchmark ranges are attributed to sources that do not state them, and the benchmark sources are missing from the bibliography.** The "most centers lie inside review ranges" claim ([paper.qmd:215](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:215)) rests on the anchors in [benchmark-comparison-labor-tax.md](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/benchmark-comparison-labor-tax.md), and the attributions are loose:
   - **ETI `[0.25, 0.5]` "Saez, Slemrod, and Giertz 2012."** SSG (2012, *JEL* 50(1):3–50) conclude the best available estimates range from `0.12` to `0.40`, midpoint `0.25`. The paper's band uses SSG's *midpoint* as its floor and a ceiling (`0.5`) that exceeds SSG's stated upper bound. Consequently the text's claim that models at `0.500/0.497/0.473` sit "at the upper edge of the Saez, Slemrod, and Giertz 2012 window" ([paper.qmd:215](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:215)) is inaccurate — those values are *above* SSG's window. A top-earner band up to `0.5` is defensible, but it should be anchored to the high-income ETI literature (Gruber–Saez 2002 ≈ `0.57` for high incomes; Saez 2004), not presented as the SSG range.
   - **Single-mother extensive margin `[0.3, 1]` "Eissa and Liebman 1996."** Eissa–Liebman (1996, *QJE*) estimate a participation *response* (up to `~2.8` pp higher LFP for single mothers after TRA86), not a participation elasticity. The `0.30` floor is the elasticity Chetty et al. (2013) back out from E&L; the upper end reflects other work (e.g., Meyer–Rosenbaum 2001). Cite the elasticity sources for an elasticity band.
   - **Income elasticity `[-0.15, -0.05]` "Imbens, Rubin, and Sacerdote 2001."** IRS (2001, *AER*) report a marginal propensity to earn out of unearned (lottery) income (`≈ -0.11`), not an income elasticity; a conversion (scaling by unearned income relative to earnings) is required. Note this, or cite an elasticity source.
   - **Capital gains `[-1, -0.2]`** has no specific citation ("Capital-gains realizations literature; medium-run tax-rate elasticity convention") and is the one row where the source column names no author. The `-0.2` (inelastic) bound is well below the persistent-realizations consensus: Dowd, McClelland & Muthitacharoen (2015, *NTJ* 68(3):511–544) estimate a persistent elasticity of `-0.72` (corrected to `-0.78` in Dowd–McClelland 2024). The wide band is not indefensible, but it should be sourced (DMM 2015; Burman–Randolph 1994; CBO/JCT conventions) and the generous `-0.2` bound justified.
   - **Bibliography.** None of SSG 2012, Eissa–Liebman 1996, Peterman 2016, Blundell–MaCurdy 1999, Imbens–Rubin–Sacerdote 2001, or DMM 2015 appears in [references.bib](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/references.bib); only McClelland–Mok (CBO 2012) and Saez 2001 are present. Since these anchors carry the paper's benchmark claim, they should be full bibliographic entries, not table strings.

**4. Several descriptive claims are contradicted by the paper's own tables/data.**
   - **"Under v4, the canonical income elasticity is negative for every model"** ([paper.qmd:257](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:257)) is false: `gemini-3.5-flash` centers at `+0.011` (positive), as the paper itself notes twenty lines earlier ([paper.qmd:215](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:215)). This reads as a claim left stale after the 17-model extension; it needs "every April model" or an explicit exception.
   - **The abstract's capital-gains sign claim** — "models return a negative w.r.t.-tax-rate elasticity paired with a positive w.r.t.-net-of-tax-rate elasticity" ([paper.qmd:12](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:12)) — overstates the audit. GPT-5.4 nano returns a *negative* net-of-tax median (`-0.2`) and is flagged "both negative" ([cap-gains-convention-audit.md:5](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/cap-gains-convention-audit.md:5)). The correct statement is "16 of 17." The body ([paper.qmd:360](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:360)) is appropriately hedged; the abstract should match it. (Relatedly, gemini-3.5-flash and GPT-5.4 nano have implied-`τ` bootstrap intervals of `[-0.889, 3.250]` and `[-3.592, 5.000]`, so their point sign-consistency masks near-total incoherence — worth a clause.)
   - **"The capital-gains realizations elasticity remains the most cross-model-dispersed canonical object"** ([paper.qmd:12](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:12)) and **"the largest spread-to-width ratio occurring for the capital gains realizations elasticity"** ([paper.qmd:217](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:217)) are contradicted by Table A6: the IES has a larger spread-to-width ratio (`0.548`) than capital gains (`0.516`), and on raw spread the Armington elasticity (`1.68`) exceeds capital gains (`0.94`) ([quantity-disagreement.md:5](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/quantity-disagreement.md:5)–[quantity-disagreement.md:7](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/quantity-disagreement.md:7)). The claim holds only within the labor-and-tax subpanel; add that qualifier or name the IES.
   - **Income-elasticity out-of-range count.** The benchmark table reports `14/17` inside `[-0.15,-0.05]` ([benchmark-comparison-labor-tax.md:6](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/benchmark-comparison-labor-tax.md:6)), i.e., three out, but the text names only two (`gemini-3.5-flash` `+0.011`, `gpt-5.5` `-0.035`). The unnamed third is `gpt-5.4-nano` at `-0.0007` — an *April* model sitting essentially at zero (verified from the model summaries). This mildly undercuts the "the clarifier eliminated the wrong-sign outliers for every April model" framing ([paper.qmd:215](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:215)): the clarifier left an April model with an economically null income elasticity. Name it and reconcile.

**Minor Comments**

- **Grok 4.20 ETI center.** The text cites Grok 4.20 at `0.497` ([paper.qmd:215](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:215)), but the artifact `pooled_point_estimate` is `0.500` and Table 4's ETI median is `0.500`. Reconcile the `0.497` figure (mixture-median vs mean-of-points?).
- **Economically implausible ETI upper tails drive the wide top-rate bands.** Table 4 ETI p95 values reach `1.38`–`1.46` (GPT-5.4 mini, GPT-5.4 nano, Claude Haiku 4.5), far above any credible top-earner ETI. These feed the `~15%` lower ends of the top-rate intervals. The propagation is faithful to the elicited distributions, but one sentence acknowledging that the wide bands partly reflect out-of-range ETI mass would help the public-finance reader.
- **Quantile repair on a sign-sensitive cell.** `gemini-3.5-flash`'s income-elasticity cell shows `n_quantile_repaired_runs = 5` (a third of runs). A near-zero center built from a cell where a third of runs needed quantile repair is less reliable; worth a note where that center is discussed.
- **Pareto parameter interpretation.** `a = 1.470` (inverted-Pareto `b = 3.13`) is a thicker tail than the usual top-*bracket* estimates (`b ≈ 2`), but it is close to the Diamond–Saez `a = 1.5`; this is reassuring and worth stating. Because the calibration uses the top-1% cutoff (`$660k`), the "top marginal rate" interpretation should be tied to that population, as the footnote already partly does ([paper.qmd:231](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:231)).
- **Broken cross-reference.** The footnote directs readers to Table A13 for robustness "to threshold choice ... [via] the Pareto parameter a" ([paper.qmd:231](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:231)), but A13 is centered at `a = 1.500` with columns `1.3/1.5/1.7` and no `1.470` — so it does not actually bracket the text's baseline. Add an `a = 1.470` column or reset the baseline.
- **Truncation is fine.** The ETI-at-zero truncation and the claim that every pooled ETI p05 exceeds `0.10` are accurate (minimum p05 is `0.101`, Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite); no change needed there ([paper.qmd:243](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd:243)).
- **Formula usage is correct.** The Saez (2001) top-rate formula `τ* = (1-ḡ)/(1-ḡ+a e)`, the sufficient-statistic `a`, the sign identity `ε_τ = -[τ/(1-τ)] ε_{1-τ}`, and its inversion `τ = -ρ/(1-ρ)` are all stated and applied correctly; the sign-convention math is sound. My concern is the welfare-weight *normalization* (Major 2) and the `a` *inconsistency* (Major 1), not the formulae themselves.

**Concrete Requests for Revision**

1. Regenerate Table 4 and Table A13 with the calibrated `a = 1.470` (`ḡ = 0.595`; `γ=2` weight `0.424`), or correct all text/footnote to `a = 1.500`; ensure the footnote, the Table 4 note, and the A13 note report a single consistent `a`, and remove or explain the "fallback" wording. Confirm the headline top-rate numbers in the text reproduce from the committed table.
2. State the welfare-weight normalization explicitly (average top marginal utility relative to the threshold earner), add a `ḡ_top → 0` Diamond–Saez / revenue-maximizing reference column, and either qualify or relabel "utilitarian" so readers are not led to read `33–44%` as the textbook utilitarian optimum.
3. Fix the benchmark attributions: anchor the top-earner ETI band to Gruber–Saez 2002 / Saez 2004 (and report SSG's actual `0.12–0.40`); attribute the single-mother elasticity to Chetty et al. 2013 / Meyer–Rosenbaum 2001; flag Imbens–Rubin–Sacerdote 2001 as an MPE; cite Dowd–McClelland–Muthitacharoen 2015 for capital gains and justify the `-0.2` bound. Add all benchmark sources to `references.bib` as full entries.
4. Correct the contradicted claims: "income elasticity negative for every model" (add exception), the abstract's capital-gains sign claim ("16 of 17"), and the "most dispersed / largest spread-to-width = capital gains" claim (qualify to labor-and-tax or name the IES). Name `gpt-5.4-nano` as the third out-of-range income-elasticity model.
5. Optional but valuable: add a sentence noting that the wide top-rate intervals partly reflect economically implausible elicited ETI upper tails, and flag the quantile-repair rate on the `gemini-3.5-flash` income-elasticity cell.

**Recommendation: Major revision.**

Summary (3 sentences): The paper's data collection is sound and the public-finance translation has genuinely improved (non-zero welfare weight, uncertainty propagation, Pareto/CRRA robustness, and a real capital-gains convention audit), but the headline optimal-top-tax exhibit is computed in the text with a microdata-calibrated Pareto parameter `a = 1.470` while the committed Table 4 and Table A13 use a hard-coded fallback `a = 1.500`, so none of the reported top-rate figures reproduce from the artifacts. The "utilitarian" welfare weight `ḡ = a/(a+γ) ≈ 0.6` is a threshold-normalized (not population-normalized) object that halves the implied optimal rate relative to the standard Diamond–Saez benchmark, and this is neither stated nor caveated. Finally, the benchmark ranges are attributed to sources that do not state them (the ETI band exceeds the actual Saez–Slemrod–Giertz 2012 window; Eissa–Liebman 1996 report a participation response, not an elasticity) and those sources are absent from the bibliography, while several descriptive claims — "income elasticity negative for every model," the capital-gains sign audit, and "capital gains is the most dispersed canonical object" — are contradicted by the paper's own tables; all of these are correctable, which is why I recommend major rather than reject.
